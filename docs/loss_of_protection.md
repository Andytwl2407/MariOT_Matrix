# Loss of Protection

# Summary
Adversaries may compromise maritime protective systems designed to prevent the effects of faults and abnormal conditions, resulting in equipment damage, prolonged disruptions, and hazards to personnel. Maritime operations often encounter rapid faults where human reaction is insufficient, making automated protection crucial. Adversaries may target and disable these systems to enable subsequent attacks or allow faults to go unchecked. Detection of compromised protection can lead to process shutdowns, causing significant productivity and revenue losses, aligning with adversaries' goals to disrupt maritime processes.

&nbsp;

# Mitigations
This type of attack technique cannot be easily mitigated with preventive controls since it is based on the abuse of system features.
